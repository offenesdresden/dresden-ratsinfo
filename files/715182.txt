"Das neue elektronische experimentelle improvisierte KONZERTs in verschiedene
ungewöhnliche Konzertsäle in Dresden (und auch andere Orte) wo manchmal auch
getanzt, gelesen, gekocht und gegessen wird“
ist der Name der Konzertreihe, die Vasily Ratmansky und Volker Sondermann seit
November 2022 im Abstand von zwei Monaten veranstalten. Dabei ndet jedes
KONZERTs an einem anderen Ort statt, wodurch Dresden als kulturelle Begegnungsstätte
erforscht und erweitert wird. Der Begri „Konzert“ ist dabei nicht ausschließlich
musikalisch zu verstehen, sondern eher vom Wortursprung her, wo es ‚zusammenwirken‘
oder ‚wetteifern‘ bedeutet. Wir referieren damit auf den ursprünglichen Ritualcharakter,
den ein Konzert in seiner Entstehungszeit im 16. Jahrhundert hatte und zugleich auf die
O enheit, dass interdisziplinäre Begegnung und gemeinschaftsstiftender Austausch
statt nden kann. So wurden in den vergangenen KONZERTsi neben musikalischen
Beiträgen auch Performances mit Elementen aus der bildenden Kunst gezeigt.

Stefan Prins
In his compositional work Prins seeks to critique
received convention, to break the framework of the
usual, and dispose of aesthetic axioms. He envisions a
musical art form beyond the safe con nes of the
»scene«, wherein the connection to the larger cultural
discourse has gotten lost. A central pre-condition for the
making of a new music with a future is the role of the
aware, critical observer, one who is prepared to exploit
the technologies and mechanisms of the prefabricated
media with a view to their possibilities for new music. –
Stefan Prins lives up to this calling. (Michael Rebhahn,
2012)
Performing improvised music is an important part of Prins's musical activities. Together
with Thomas Olbrechts & Joachim Devillé he was one of the founders of the long-standing
collectief reFLEXible‚ an ensemble that focuses exclusively on free improvised or instant
composed music, often including dance, performance, video, lm, installation.

Mazen Kerbaj is a Lebanese comics
author, visual artist, and musician born
in Beirut in 1975. He also works on
selective illustration and design
projects and has taught at the
American University of Beirut.
Kerbaj is the author of 15 books
translated into more than ten
languages and his work has been
shown in galleries, museums and art
fairs around the world.

Mazen Kerbaj is widely considered as one of the initiators and key players of the Lebanese
free improvisation and experimental music scene. As a trumpet player, he pushes the
boundaries of the instrument beyond recognition.

Since 2009 the composers Martin Recker (1991) and Paul Hauptmeier (1993) have been
working together as an artist duo in the eld of sound- and multimedia art.
Together they studied composition with Robin Minard and Maximilian Marcoll at the
Hochschule für Musik FRANZ LISZT Weimar, as well as at the “University of California San
Diego” with Katharina Rosenberger, Natacha Diels and Miller Puckette (Paul Hauptmeier)
and at the Sibelius Academy Hesinki with Marianne Decoster-Taivalkoski (Martin Recker).
In addition to works for theater and opera, live electronics, radio and electro-acoustic
music, their focus lies on sound installations in public spaces.
They are co-founders and board members of the ZiMMT – Center for Immersive Media
Art, Music and Technology – in Leipzig. There they conduct research in the eld of spatial
audio and organize workshops, panels, concerts and exhibitions on the subject.
